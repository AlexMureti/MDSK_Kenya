Accessibility

Access is not a feature. It is the standard.

MDSK's website is built to be fast, readable, keyboard-friendly, and easier to leave quickly when a visitor is viewing sensitive information.

Use MDSK GuideReport an access issue

Use the site your way

  • Keyboard navigationMove through pages with a keyboard, visible focus, and a skip link to main content.
  • Readable controlsIncrease text size, switch to high contrast, or use readable mode from the top bar.
  • Reduced motionThe site respects reduced-motion preferences and keeps movement restrained.
  • Lower data loadImages are compressed so pages remain practical on mobile connections.

Language and communication

  • KiswahiliKiswahili routes help more visitors understand MDSK's mission and contact options.
  • Easy ReadPlain-language summaries make key information easier to scan and understand.
  • Kenyan Sign LanguageMDSK highlights KSL access at events and can add KSL summaries as public clips become available.
  • Direct helpVisitors who meet a barrier can contact MDSK so a human team member can respond.
Sensitive pathways

A safer route for personal health, safety, and support questions.

For GBV, SRHR, HIV, mental health, safety, or private support questions, visitors can use direct MDSK contact routes instead of sharing personal details in the guide.

Quick exit

The quick-exit button sends the visitor away from the site immediately. It appears in the top bar on every page.

Access issue?

Email info@menwdsocietyk.org and describe the page, device, browser, and barrier encountered.
